Desk-based Assessment of Deforestation

In this section, the deforestation area is verified through visual interpretation, using deforestation alerts and the most recent satellite imagery as references. After verification, the location data of the confirmed deforestation area is sent to the local representative for field survey.

Deforestation Alert and Satellite Imagery

PDMS uses the Google earth engine and GEE Data Downloader to distribute Deforestation Alerts to each province once a week. Simultaneously, it also delivers composite images from Sentinel-2 (https://developers.google.com/earth-engine/datasets/catalog/COPERNICUS_S2_SR_HARMONIZED) taken over the last month, as well as Sentinel-2 images taken in the previous week. This allows for a cross-check by visually comparing the forest cover conditions of the previous month with those of the last week to verify if deforestation is actually occurring, based on the Deforestation Alerts.

Since Sentinel-2 is an optical satellite image, it has some limitations, such as difficulties in observing the forest cover due to cloud, haze, and moist in mountainous areas, especially during the rainy season. Therefore, in addition to Sentinel-2 images, PDMS also automatically distribute monthly mosaics of higher resolution images of the NICFI Satellite Data Program Basemaps (https://developers.google.com/earth-engine/datasets/catalog/projects_planet-nicfi_assets_basemaps_asia ) once a month. This also delivers images from last month and the current month, respectively. By comparing these high-resolution images with less cloud cover, it is possible to examine forest conditions in greater detail

The differences between Sentinel2 and NICFI Planet images are explained below.

Sentinel-2

  • Lower Resolution (10 m)

  • Every 5 days (almost Weekly)

  • The frequency of shooting is higher and Sentinel 2 is used for weekly forest change detection in PDMS.

  • On the other hand, images are often unclear due to lower resolution, clouds and haze.

NICFI Planet (Level 1)

  • Higher Resolution (4.77 m)

  • Every Month (level1)

  • Tend to get a clear image because the Planet image are composited from multiple images taken within a month.

  • However, the frequency of shooting is not high (one month).

  • It is useful for verification of deforestation area detected by PDMS.

Verify Deforestation Area

The Deforestation Alert indicates the likelihood of deforestation, but there is an uncertainty in the result due to misinterpretation of satellite imagery caused by the clouds, haze and leaf-fall. The PDMS Web application helps to verify deforestation areas by using Deforestation Alert and satellite imagery.

Additionally, while the Deforestation Alert covers the entire province, it is not feasible to monitor all deforestation areas within a province due to human and budgetary constraints at the provincial and district levels. Therefore, it is important to first identify the key monitoring areas (e.g., national parks, conservation areas, or village conservation and protection forests) in collaboration with stakeholders. After identifying these target areas, upload the GIS data (KMZ file) of the monitoring target area to the PDMS Web application to monitor deforestation within these areas.

  1. Zoom in to the target monitoring area.

  1. Confirm whether the Deforestation Alert is occurring within the target monitoring area.

  1. Use the Deforestation Alert toggle and slider controls to verify whether deforestation has occurred.

Register Deforestation Area for Field Survey

Survey sites are identified based on the deforestation alert processed by Google Earth Engine .

  1. Click on polygon icon that appears to the right of hand icon. Select this icon to identify the deforestation area to be surveyed. The hand icon is also used to move the satellite image up, down, left or right.

  1. The deforestation alert processed by GEE is shown in the figure below. Using satellite imagery (Sentinel 2, NICFI Planet) as a reference image, the deforestation alert needs to verify whether deforestation is indeed occurring, before selecting the area to be surveyed.

  1. Click on each corner along the boundary of the deforestation area, and double-click the last point to create and register the polygon.

  1. After defining the polygon, new screen automatically appears showing the plot number, area and data source as follows.

You will select the following data source.

  1. Select survey sites based on GEE analysis results.

  2. Select survey sites based on the request of the site.

  1. After selecting the data source, click Registration to register the survey site in the database. If you click Cancel before clicking Registration, you will return to the previous screen without registration.

A pin icon is displayed on the map screen at the registered location.

  1. Click the [Plot Data] tab to display a list of survey sites. You can see the registered site in the list here.
