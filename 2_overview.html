PDMS Overview

Background

Agricultural expansion, shifting cultivation, infrastructure developments and uncontrolled logging are the key drivers of deforestation in Laos. To address deforestation, Laos has taken mitigation measures, such as delineation of conservation, protection, production forest area, land use planning and forest management planning in the field, however, the forest monitoring has not been effectively implemented to ensure the proper forest management. Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry issued Prime Minister Order No.11 in June 2023 to ensure strictness n the management, protection, development and utilization of forests and forestlands. For the forest monitoring and forest law enforcement, Laos has been piloting and implementing satellite-based forest monitoring with the support from development partners. One of them is the near real-time Provincial Deforestation Monitoring System (PDMS) which combines near-real time satellite-based analysis and field survey to monitor land use changes in the target forest areas. The PDMS has been developed by the JICA/F-REDD project since 2018, and pilot activities have been carried out in two provinces (Luang Prabang and Oudomxay) until 2022. It has since been used as a deforestation monitoring tool in the forestry project supported by the World Bank, Germany, NGOs and other donors, and has been scaled up to their target provinces. Recognizing the expansion of PDMS deployment, the Lao Government recognized its effectiveness and practicability and, accredited the PDMS as an official forest monitoring tool in Laos in May 2024 (No.2761/MAF, May 24 2024).

Purpose

  • Enhance forest monitoring and forest law enforcement to control illegal land conversion

  • Provide more accurate forest change data for effective and efficient forest monitoring

Forest Change

Deforestation: Forest lands are converted into agricultural (or other kinds of) lands (<0.2 ha).

Target Forest Areas

“Official” forest areas where the boundaries are clearly identified by GIS data, such as; * Conservation, Protection and Production Forest Area * Village forest area * Land concession area (Adjustable to a user-defined area of interest)

Operation Period and Frequency

Every week during dry season (Adjustable in a user-defined duration)

PDMS System Component

The PDMS consists of a Web Application and Mobile Applications. The former runs on a browser on the laptop/desktop PC and the latter on a mobile device.

PDMS Operation Workflow

The workflow of this system operation is shown below.

Operation Flow Diagram

Deforestation Alert

  • GEE (Google Earth Engine) Data Downloader Tool of the Web Application automatically provides weekly deforestation alerts and associated satellite imagery in a given target province.

  • A machine learning script for deforestation detection running on Google Earth Engine (GEE) derives deforestation alerts by performing the direct change classification approach using predictor variables generated by two images (Sentinel-2) from different observation periods. This script already covers the entire Lao territory and enables to identify potential deforestation areas for a specified period and target area.

Desk-based Assessment

  • PDMS operator (PAFO officer) conduct desk-based assessment to verify the certainty of forest loss based on the deforestation alert, in the web application.

  • Process of desk assessment is as follows.

    1. Confirm that the deforestation alert using GEE data downloader tool are displayed in the Web Application;

    2. Assess and verify the certainty of forest loss based on deforestation alert and satellite imagery (Sentinel 2 and NICFI Planet); and

    3. Mark the defined deforestation areas and send them to the field officers (DAFO) with request for field survey

Field Monitoring

  • The field officer (DAFO) use a mobile device (tablet or mobile phone) to go to field survey and collect on-site information in the deforestation areas requested by the PDMS operator (PAFO).

  • The mobile device applications consist of:

    1. Map application (OruxMaps) which navigates the field officers to the defined deforestation areas and;

    2. Data collection application (ODK collect) , which allows web-based reporting of the field survey results

  • In addition, the field officer (DAFO) can independently report the deforestation areas through the mobile device application without a request from the PDMS operator (PAFO), such as through their field observations or based on reports from farmers.

  • After submitting the field data, the field officer (DAFO) will inform the relevant organizations for the necessary action.

Reporting and Dashboard

  • The field survey data including spatial and attribute information on deforestation areas submitted via mobile device applications are semi-automatically transferred to the National Forest Monitoring System (NFMS) server.

  • This quantitative and qualitative data can be exported for the reporting to the relevant organization.

  • PDMS Dashboard explores interactive charts and tables that summarize key statistics on deforestation in Laos and can be used to make evidence-based decision.

PDMS Implementation Structure

The roles of respective organizations for the implementation of the PDMS are summarized below:

National level

Department of Forestry

  • Develop regulations and guidance related to the PDMS, and set up institutional arrangement for forest monitoring in coordination with DOFI

  • Coordinate with the Forestry Section of PAFO in planning and implementation of the PDMS

  • Provide capacity building and technical support for the PDMS implementation.

  • As a system administrator, maintain and update the web application and database of the PDMS in the FIPD database server.

  • Review, compile and report the result of forest monitoring and periodically report to the relevant organization at National level

Department of Forest Inspection

  • Develop regulations and guidance related to the PMDS, and set up institutional arrangement for forest monitoring in coordination with DOF

  • Coordinate with the Forest Inspection Section of PAFO in planning and implementation of the PDMS

  • Provide capacity building and technical support for the PDMS implementation.

  • Review, compile and report the result of forest monitoring and periodically report to the relevant organization at National level

Provincial level

Forestry Section in Provincial Agriculture and Forestry Office

  • As a PDMS operating entity, identify the deforestation areas and request relevant DAFO for field–check periodically using the web application.

  • Coordinate with DAFO in planning and implementation of the PDMS.

  • Disseminate the technical knowledge and skills for PDMS operation and implementation to DAFO.

  • Review and compile the result of forest monitoring, and report to the relevant organization at Provincial level

  • Provide necessary administrative measures to the offenders in accordance with the level of the violation

Forest Inspection Section in Provincial Agriculture and Forestry Office

  • As a PDMS operating entity, identify the deforestation area and request relevant DAFO for field–check periodically using the web application.

  • Coordinate with DAFO in planning and implementation of PDMS.

  • Disseminate the technical knowledge and skills for PDMS operation and implementation to DAFO.

  • Review and compile the result of forest monitoring, and report to the relevant organization at Provincial level

  • Provide necessary administrative measures and impose penalties to the offenders in accordance with the level of the violation

District level

District Agriculture and Forestry Office

  • As a forest monitoring entity, conduct field–check and report the status of deforestation areas by using the mobile applications.

  • Formulate the plan and implement for forest monitoring.

  • Disseminate the technical knowledge and skills for PDMS implementation in DAFO.

  • Review and compile the result of forest monitoring, and report to the relevant organization at District level.

  • Provide necessary measures and impose penalties for offenders in accordance with the level of the violation.
