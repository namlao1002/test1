Field Monitoring

Once deforestation sites are identified by the PDMS web application, Field Officer will conduct forest monitoring activities by visiting the sites where deforestation has occurred, interviewing villagers to gather necessary information, and reporting the results of the field survey. To conduct field surveys efficiently, PDMS uses two applications. One is Oruxmaps, a map application that displays deforestation areas to facilitate access to the site, and the other is ODK Collect, a survey form application that simplifies data collection in the field. These applications should be installed on your mobile phone or tablet to carry out the field surveys.

Applications used in these field surveys must not be downloaded from the Google Play Store, but rather from dedicated websites. To download application, certain configurations need to be completed in advance.

Oruxmaps

Oruxmaps is a mapping application designed to support field surveys, particularly for forest monitoring. It displays specific locations of forest degradation and related geographic information, helping forest officers easily access these sites. Users can install Oruxmaps on their mobile phones or tablets, and it works offline, making it useful in areas without internet access. The app is an essential tool for verifying on-site forest conditions and conducting efficient field surveys based on location data.

  1. Go to the next screen by swiping to the left. If you selected Plot ID allocated on the previous screen, enter the ID number in this question highlighted in red box. (If you selected Not Plot ID allocated on the previous screen, this question will not appear.)

Measurement of Deforestation Area

ODK Collect enables the measurement of deforestation areas using the GPS function of a mobile device. In the question titled Measurement of Deforestation Area, you are presented with two options: Field Measurement or Measurement using drone or satellite image.

Field Measurement (1) Select Field Measurement and move to the next screen, and tap Get polygon red frame below.

  1. If you have already reached the deforestation area to be measured, you can view a map of your surrounding area on your mobile device. Tap flag icon highlighted in red frame and select Manual location recording in the box Input method, then tap start

  1. Walk along the deforestation area in the field and tap Record a point at each corner. The GPS information from the mobile device will then create a polygon representing the deforested area on the map, as shown below.

  2. After completing the walk around the deforestation area and tap Record a point at each corner, tap the icon highlighted in red. The recorded GPS coordinates will be displayed. If you tap “Next,” the area you have covered will be shown and automatically entered into the form.

Measurement using drone or satellite image (5) Select Measurement using drone or satellite image and move to the next screen., Here, you will manually enter the area of deforestation based on the results of measurements using aerial photographs taken by drone or satellite images, or on visual estimates by field observation.

Geo-point (Location) of Deforestation Area

  1. Tap Get Point then you can see the map in your location when you specify the geo-location by holding down on the point, a balloon will appear. Tap the area highlighted in red frame to save your current geo-location.

  1. Latitude and longtitude, and attribute data can be saved.

Picture of Deforestation Area

Tap “Take Photo” (red frame) to activate the camera and take a picture. Tap “Choose Image” (blue frame) to select an image saved on the tablet.

Forms End Screen

Forms end with a standard screen that displays the name of the filled form and options to save as draft or indicate that the form is ready to send.

  • If you tap the Save as draft button, the form will be saved and available for more editing from the Drafts screen.

  • If you tap on the Finalize button marks the form as Finalized which makes it available from the Ready to send screen and prevents further editing.

Edit Drafts

When filling out a form, you can save it as a draft and edit it later.

Send Finalized Forms

You will find finalized forms in the Ready to send list, displayed by the name that the form definition specifies. When there are forms that are ready to send, you will see a blue notification badge on the Ready to send button and its title will become bold. Check the form checkbox on the right side and tap the Send selected button. After a short time, the Upload Results box will appear, displaying the file name and Success if it was successfully sent to the server.

PDMS Web Application

Final Review of the Result of Field Survey

Once the field survey is completed and the results are sent to the server, the PDMS operator will carry out a final review of the survey results. If any deficiencies are found, the Field Officer may be requested to conduct a re-survey.

  1. Click Monitoring Plot box

  2. Select Completed (finish field investigation) in the Display options box. You can see only the surveyed plots of Completed (finish field investigation) in the map.

  3. Select the survey plots that you want to review and double-click the survey plot.

  4. After review the result of fiel survey,

  • If no resurvey is required, click Do not conduct re-servey to complete the survey.

  • If a re-investigation is needed, click Conduct re-servey to request a re-investigation.

If you click Do not conduct re-servey and return to the previous screen, the process column will be updated from Completed (finish field investigation) to Completed (including field investigation), indicating that you have reviewed and accepted the result by PDMS Operator.

Delete Data

(1) When you select Plot List then click a row, or select Map then click a survey site on the map, the survey results will be displayed. You can delete this survey location by clicking Delete Data.
